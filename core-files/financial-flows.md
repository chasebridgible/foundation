# Financial flows and economics

## Why this lane matters

Financial flows describe **how cash enters and leaves**, **what drives profitability**, and **where surprises originate**. This anchors prioritization in reality—especially for automation and delegation decisions.

---

## Questions

1. **Walk through how a dollar travels** from customer intent to collected cash—including deposits, invoicing, timing gaps, refunds, and bad debt—where does money stall?

2. What are your **primary revenue streams**, and how concentrated are they across customers, products, seasons, or regions?

3. What **cost buckets** dominate—people, COGS, rent, marketing, financing—and which have been unusually volatile lately?

4. Where is **margin created and destroyed** in a typical engagement (rework, discounts, scope creep, utilization, spoilage, warranty)?

5. What **financial reports** do you actually use to steer the business—and what questions can’t you answer today with the data you trust?

6. What **tradeoffs** do you routinely make between growth, profit, cash safety, and stress—and which one tends to lose when you’re busy?

7. What **financial risks** keep you awake—concentration, debt, covenant, FX, commodity, insurance, regulatory—and what early warning signs do you watch?
