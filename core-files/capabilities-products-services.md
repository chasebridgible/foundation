# Capabilities, products, and services

## Why this lane matters

This lane maps **what you sell**, **what you must be capable of to deliver**, and **where delivery strains**. That distinction matters for prioritizing automation, partnerships, hiring, and scope discipline.

---

## Questions

1. **What do customers actually purchase**—named offerings, customized engagements, bundles—and how cleanly do those map to how work gets scheduled and staffed?

2. For each major offering, **what does “done well” require** in terms of skills, assets, approvals, timing, and coordination—and where does reality fall short?

3. Where is **margin strongest and weakest**, and what drives that—is it complexity, rework, procurement, utilization, pricing discipline, or something else?

4. What parts of delivery are **repeatable** versus genuinely **custom every time**, and where does the sales team promise customization that operations experiences as chaos?

5. What **capabilities live outside the firm** today—vendors, contractors, platforms—and where would losing one vendor hurt fastest?

6. What offerings are **legacy** but still demanded, **new** but unproven, or **aspirational** but not operationally owned by anyone?

7. If you stopped offering one thing tomorrow, **what downstream effects** would ripple through customers, cash, team workload, and reputation?
