# Organizational structure and roles

## Why this lane matters

This lane captures **who exists in the organization**, **who reports to whom**, and **what each role is accountable for**. Clarity here is prerequisite to routing work, permissions, and automation without ambiguity.

---

## Questions

1. **How would you draw the org** as it exists today—not the chart you wish you had—including contractors, fractional leaders, and key vendors who behave like insiders?

2. For each critical function (sales, delivery, finance, ops, admin, HR, IT, compliance), **who owns outcomes** versus who merely assists—and where is ownership fuzzy?

3. Where do **informal influence** and **formal authority** diverge—and who do people actually go to when something urgent breaks?

4. What **roles are overloaded**, which feel **underutilized**, and which exist mostly for historical reasons?

5. What decisions or tasks **still require the owner or a specific person**—and what happens when that person is unavailable?

6. What **handoffs** between people or teams are the most brittle today—where does work bounce, stall, or get dropped?

7. If you hired for **one missing role** next quarter with the highest leverage, what would that person own on day thirty and day ninety?
