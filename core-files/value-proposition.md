# Value proposition

## Why this lane matters

The value proposition is **who you serve**, **what problem you solve**, **why they choose you**, and **what they’d miss if you disappeared**. Without this, automation amplifies the wrong work and messaging sounds interchangeable.

---

## Questions

1. **Who is the primary customer** you’re built for—and who often buys anyway even though they’re not an ideal fit?

2. If a thoughtful outsider watched your business for a week, **what job would they say customers hire you for**—functional, emotional, risk-reduction, speed, certainty?

3. What **promise do you implicitly make** that isn’t written anywhere—and where do you feel most exposed if that promise breaks?

4. What do customers say **almost bought them**, or **almost lost them**, in recent deals—especially language they used in their own words?

5. What **alternatives** do prospects seriously consider before choosing you—not only named competitors, but “do nothing,” in-house, substitutes, or different categories?

6. What **proof** makes someone believe you’ll deliver—references, demos, guarantees, track record, process transparency—and where is proof weakest today?

7. Where does **pricing or packaging** signal something important about value—and where does it create confusion or friction?
