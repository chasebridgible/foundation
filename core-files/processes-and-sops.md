# Processes and standard work

## Why this lane matters

Processes are the **repeatable spine** of operations: what is documented, what lives in muscle memory, and what should not be improvised. This separates what you can safely template or automate from what truly needs craft.

---

## Questions

1. Which processes are **stable and repeated often enough** that they deserve a clear standard—and which are inherently one-offs?

2. What do you actually **document** today (checklists, playbooks, training), where does that documentation **drift from reality**, and what never gets written because “everyone knows”?

3. Where does **training** happen formally versus by shadowing—and what breaks when the trainer isn’t around?

4. What steps **almost always cause friction**—missing inputs, unclear definitions of done, approvals, quality gates—and what workarounds did people invent?

5. Where does **quality** get validated—and where does rework originate?

6. Which processes **touch the customer** directly—and where does internal mess become visible externally?

7. If you could **freeze one process** exactly as your best performer does it today and scale it, which process would move the needle most?
