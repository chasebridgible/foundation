# Time management and attention

## Why this lane matters

Calendar and task lists rarely show **where leverage leaks**. This lane captures **what actually consumes hours**—including interruptions, rework, emotional labor, and “quick checks”—for owners and key employees. That picture anchors delegation, automation, and operating-model redesign.

---

## Questions

### Owner / leader lens

1. **Walk through a typical week**—what categories of activity swallowed the most hours (customer-facing work, finance, hiring, firefighting, communication), and what feels urgent but rarely moves the business forward?

2. What repeatedly lands on **your desk because others aren’t sure they’re allowed to decide**, **lack information**, or **it’s emotionally easier to escalate**—and what would “handled without you” look like?

3. Where does **context-switching** hurt most—meetings, messages, inbound calls, shifting between strategic and operational work—and what patterns trigger it?

### Employee / operator lens

4. For **one or two roles that matter most right now**, where does their time go in practice versus where leadership assumes it goes—and what surprises people when they track it honestly?

5. What work **looks productive** (busy inbox, motion, multitasking) but **doesn’t change outcomes**—and what low-visibility work is actually critical?

### Shared dynamics

6. What **interruptions** are predictable—who pulls whom, through what channels—and which interruptions signal a broken process versus healthy collaboration?

7. If you reclaimed **five focused hours per week** for the owner (or for a bottleneck role), **what would you stop, delegate, automate, batch, or redesign first**—and what scares you about each option?
