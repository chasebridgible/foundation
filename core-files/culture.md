# Company culture

## Why this lane matters

Culture is **how decisions get made when the manual is silent**—norms around quality, urgency, candor, blame, learning, and customer care. It shapes what automation and delegation can safely do without damaging trust.

---

## Questions

1. **When things go wrong** in front of a customer, what does the organization do by instinct—cover, disclose, escalate, absorb cost, argue—and what stories do people tell about those moments?

2. What behaviors get **rewarded** here in practice (promotion, praise, slack, forgiveness) versus what gets **punished**—including subtle signals?

3. How do people **give and receive feedback**, and where does conflict tend to go underground?

4. What **rituals or rhythms** shape how people work together—meetings, standups, reviews, celebrations—and which rituals feel productive versus theatrical?

5. What does **“good enough”** mean in different departments—and where do standards silently differ?

6. Where does **trust** live today—individual heroes, documented process, supervision, tooling—and where does mistrust slow everything down?

7. If you hired ten more people next year, **what cultural traits must scale** versus **what might break** at larger scale?
