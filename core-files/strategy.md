# Strategy

## Why this lane matters

Strategy here means **where the business is headed**, **what tradeoffs are acceptable**, and **which bets are implicit** in day-to-day choices—not a polished slide deck. Capturing it grounds automation and delegation in intent, not only tasks.

---

## Questions

1. **Three to five years out**, what would “this worked” look like for the business—and what outcomes would you be disappointed to realize you optimized for by accident?

2. **What are you deliberately choosing not to do** (markets, customer types, products, geographies), and what pressures tend to erode those boundaries?

3. Where does strategy actually get decided today—in **your head**, **conversations**, **written plans**, **investors or advisors**, **financial constraints**—and where does lived behavior diverge from what gets said?

4. What **core bets** are you making about customers, competitors, regulation, talent, or technology that people inside the company might not all name the same way?

5. What **constraints are non-negotiable** (risk, reputation, ethics, cash, quality bar, geographic footprint), and where have those constraints been tested recently?

6. When priorities collide—for example growth versus margin, speed versus quality—which **tie-breakers** do you want the organization to use when you’re not in the room?

7. What **external shifts** (market, tech, labor, supply chain, policy) would force you to rethink the plan fastest—and what early signals would you watch for?
