# Goals — business, personal, and horizons

## Why this lane matters

Goals reveal **priorities**, **time horizons**, and **tradeoffs**. Owners often carry goals split across business outcomes and personal life—both influence where leverage matters and what “success” means for redesign work.

---

## Questions

### Near-term and longer horizons

1. Looking **twelve months ahead**, what outcomes must be true for you to consider this period a success—and what would feel like failure even if revenue looked fine?

2. On a **three-to-five-year horizon**, what are you trying to prove—market position, operational stability, succession, lifestyle, wealth, mission—and what uncertainty worries you most?

3. Which goals are **explicit and written** versus **assumed but never said out loud**—and who else in the organization knows them?

### Business goals

4. What **three to five metrics** would you trust to tell you the business is healthy—and which numbers do people game, distrust, or ignore?

5. Where are you **intentionally investing** ahead of returns (people, capability, markets) versus **harvesting**—and where does the business do the opposite by accident?

### Personal goals (especially owner / key leader)

6. What do you **personally want your week to feel like** in twelve months—time, stress, travel, types of work—and what currently makes that impossible?

7. What parts of your role **energize you** and **drain you**, and which drains are you willing to eliminate even if someone else does them “differently”?

8. What **non-negotiables** exist in your life (health, family, geography) that strategy and operations must respect?
