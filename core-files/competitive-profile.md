# Competitive profile

## Why this lane matters

Competition isn’t only **named rivals**; it’s **any way a customer solves the problem**. Clarity here focuses differentiation, pricing logic, and where you’re fighting the wrong battle.

---

## Questions

1. When you **win a deal**, what were the **top two reasons** the customer gave—or, if unstated, what do you believe mattered most?

2. When you **lose**, what do losses have in common—price, speed, trust, scope, reputation, geography, capability gaps?

3. Who do you **actually run into** in competitive situations—and who feels like competition in your head but rarely shows up in real opportunities?

4. What do **substitutes** look like for your customer—in-house hires, spreadsheets, informal networks, adjacent products—and when are those substitutes “good enough”?

5. What is **hard for others to copy** about you today—and what parts of your advantage depend on **specific people**, relationships, or tacit craft?

6. Where does **pricing** live relative to the market—and is that position deliberate or accidental?

7. Looking ahead **two years**, where could competition come from that doesn’t look threatening today—technology, consolidation, regulation, labor availability?
