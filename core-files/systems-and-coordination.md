# Systems knowledge and coordination

## Why this lane matters

“Systems” here means **how work and information actually move**—queues, handoffs, dependencies, and feedback loops—not only software. This is often where leverage hides for automation and better routing.

---

## Questions

1. **End to end**, how does value get delivered from first customer touch through completion and follow-up—where are the longest waits and the most rework?

2. Where does **information get created once** and then **re-keyed, re-explained, or lost**—and who pays the tax for that?

3. What **queues** exist (inboxes, tickets, schedules, approvals)—which are healthy buffers and which are symptoms of overload or unclear ownership?

4. What **dependencies** outside your control routinely affect delivery—suppliers, regulators, partners, weather, capacity—and how do you adapt when they slip?

5. Where do **exceptions and edge cases** cluster—custom work, fragile customers, rush jobs—and how does the system flex without breaking promises?

6. What **feedback loops** tell you early that quality, cost, or timing is drifting—and which problems you only discover late?

7. If you redesigned **one coordination point** tomorrow for maximum leverage—not maximum comfort—what would it be?
