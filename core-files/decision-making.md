# Decision-making and authority

## Why this lane matters

Decisions are where **judgment lives**. Documenting how choices are made—and when the owner must be present—turns tacit governance into something others and systems can respect.

---

## Questions

1. **What categories of decisions** can only happen with your input today—and which ones do people think need you but actually don’t?

2. Walk me through a **recent hard call**—what information was available, who was consulted, what tradeoffs were weighed, and what would have changed the outcome?

3. Where do **approval chains** speed things up versus slow them down—and where do bottlenecks feel like trust issues versus information issues?

4. What **rules of thumb** do experienced people use that newer people don’t know—and where do those heuristics sometimes misfire?

5. When two priorities conflict—for example customer urgency versus safety, margin versus relationship—**who is allowed to decide**, and how is that communicated?

6. What **exceptions** happen often enough that they should probably be policy—but aren’t written anywhere?

7. If you wanted the business to run **two weeks without you** on operational decisions, what would need to be spelled out first?
