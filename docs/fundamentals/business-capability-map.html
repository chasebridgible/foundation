Business Capability Map

A concise reference for the common capabilities most businesses must handle and the common jobs that make those capabilities real.

Format: HTML-native Authoring: edit this HTML file Status: Draft working map Use: intake classification and coverage reference

How to read this

A capability is a business ability stated as the result it must reliably produce. A job is recurring work that creates, operates, or improves that capability.

This document is not the business-specific map. It is the starting reference an agent or operator can use during intake to ask which common jobs exist, how the business handles them, and which areas are not yet understood.

Actors, tools, detailed process steps, source records, gaps, and business-specific variants belong in the intake workspace, job specs, source registry, and visible business graph.

Rule: classify information by the capability and job it affects before deciding whether the work belongs to a person, vendor, software system, agent, or mixed workflow.

The families are practical groupings. They are not departments and they do not imply one team, role, or tool.

Direction

01

Set direction and priorities

strategy
  • Define goals, time horizons, and business constraints.
  • Choose markets, customers, business model, and positioning.
  • Set operating-period priorities and success criteria.
  • Allocate time, money, people, attention, and tools.
  • Review progress and reset priorities when reality changes.
02

Make and record decisions

governance
  • Define decision rights, approvals, and escalation paths.
  • Run operating reviews, planning meetings, and exception reviews.
  • Record important decisions, rationale, and expected follow-up.
  • Delegate decisions with clear constraints and review points.
  • Retire or revise decisions that no longer fit the business.

Value and offer

03

Understand customers and markets

market
  • Research customers, prospects, lost buyers, and alternatives.
  • Define customer segments, use cases, pains, desires, and triggers.
  • Analyze competitors, substitutes, category norms, and market constraints.
  • Capture objections, buying criteria, and customer language.
  • Translate market learning into offer, product, and messaging changes.
04

Design offers people can buy

offer
  • Define the customer, promise, scope, inclusions, and exclusions.
  • Package products, services, bundles, subscriptions, or service levels.
  • Set price, payment terms, discounts, guarantees, and qualification rules.
  • Validate that the offer can be sold and fulfilled.
  • Maintain the offer catalog, menus, pages, or sales materials.
05

Create or source products and services

product
  • Design, build, source, license, assemble, or partner for what is sold.
  • Manage product, service, assortment, catalog, or roadmap changes.
  • Maintain specifications, standards, requirements, and launch readiness.
  • Verify availability, fit, quality, cost, margin, and constraints.
  • Modify, replace, or retire products and services when needed.

Reach and sell

06

Build trust and generate demand

demand
  • Manage brand, reputation, public credibility, reviews, and testimonials.
  • Choose channels, partners, locations, platforms, and market access paths.
  • Create campaigns, promotions, content, events, referrals, or outbound activity.
  • Manage marketing budgets, calendars, assets, and channel performance.
  • Track demand sources, qualified attention, and customer response.
07

Convert demand into orders

sales
  • Receive, qualify, and prioritize leads or opportunities.
  • Run sales conversations, demos, visits, checkout, or account planning.
  • Prepare proposals, bids, quotes, estimates, and order forms.
  • Resolve objections, terms, approvals, and purchase friction.
  • Record the sale result and hand off to delivery or onboarding.

Deliver and support

08

Start customer work cleanly

intake
  • Collect order, account, customer, scheduling, and requirement details.
  • Confirm scope, expectations, permissions, constraints, and next steps.
  • Set up accounts, projects, jobs, tickets, subscriptions, or service records.
  • Route work to the right owner, team, vendor, or system.
  • Handle early exceptions before delivery begins.
09

Deliver products and services reliably

fulfillment
  • Plan capacity, schedules, materials, inventory, and delivery resources.
  • Produce, assemble, perform, ship, install, serve, or complete the work.
  • Coordinate handoffs, dependencies, logistics, and field or service operations.
  • Manage changes, shortages, delays, substitutions, and delivery exceptions.
  • Close completed work and preserve the operating record.
10

Support and retain customers

customer
  • Receive and route customer questions, issues, complaints, and requests.
  • Resolve service problems, product problems, billing issues, and exceptions.
  • Manage returns, warranties, recalls, refunds, or service recovery.
  • Renew, retain, expand, refer, or reactivate customer relationships.
  • Turn repeated customer issues into product, process, or offer changes.

People and workforce

11

Hire, train, and assign people

people
  • Plan workforce needs, roles, responsibilities, and capacity.
  • Recruit, select, hire, onboard, and train people.
  • Define jobs, expectations, skills, scorecards, and ownership.
  • Manage performance, coaching, employee relations, and role changes.
  • Communicate policies, changes, expectations, and operating context.
12

Pay and administer people reliably

payroll
  • Maintain employee, contractor, classification, and benefit records.
  • Collect time, salary, commission, bonus, deduction, and reimbursement inputs.
  • Approve hours, run payroll, submit taxes or filings, and resolve corrections.
  • Manage expenses, advances, corporate cards, and workforce reimbursements.
  • Redeploy, retire, terminate, or offboard people cleanly.

Money, assets, and inputs

13

Collect, record, and control money

finance
  • Invoice, charge, collect, reconcile, and resolve payment exceptions.
  • Record revenue, expenses, payables, receivables, and general accounting.
  • Forecast and manage cash, treasury, debt, investments, and banking.
  • Pay vendors, taxes, debt, owners, and other obligations on time.
  • Maintain financial controls, reporting, close, audit, and fraud response.
14

Buy and manage outside inputs

suppliers
  • Select suppliers, vendors, tools, contractors, agencies, and partners.
  • Negotiate terms, contracts, pricing, service levels, and responsibilities.
  • Place orders, track delivery, receive inputs, and approve invoices.
  • Manage substitutions, shortages, lead times, returns, and vendor issues.
  • Review supplier performance, concentration risk, quality, and cost.
15

Maintain assets, facilities, and inventory

assets
  • Track inventory, equipment, vehicles, property, devices, and other assets.
  • Plan replenishment, storage, movement, maintenance, and replacement.
  • Acquire, construct, lease, repair, inspect, and dispose of assets.
  • Maintain facilities, workspace, access, safety, uptime, and readiness.
  • Manage asset records, warranties, cost, ownership, and loss prevention.

Systems, risk, and relationships

16

Run technology and data reliably

technology
  • Select, implement, integrate, and support tools and systems.
  • Manage access, permissions, credentials, backups, and user support.
  • Define data sources of truth, field meanings, reports, and data quality rules.
  • Maintain system reliability, security, resilience, and change control.
  • Retire or replace tools that create drag, risk, or hidden work.
17

Manage risk, compliance, and continuity

protection
  • Identify risks, obligations, permits, licenses, filings, and policies.
  • Maintain controls, insurance, safety practices, and compliance calendars.
  • Handle audits, incidents, claims, disputes, breaches, and regulatory changes.
  • Plan business continuity, disaster recovery, security, and emergency response.
  • Train people on critical controls, escalation paths, and required behavior.
18

Manage external relationships

relationships
  • Manage lender, investor, shareholder, board, and advisor relationships.
  • Manage government, regulator, trade, industry, and community relationships.
  • Manage legal counsel, contracts, intellectual property, and disputes.
  • Manage media, public relations, reputation events, and public statements.
  • Coordinate partnerships and outside relationships that affect operations.

Improve the system

19

Improve work, quality, and operating memory

learning
  • Define and maintain processes, standards, checklists, and quality requirements.
  • Manage projects, portfolios, improvement work, and change adoption.
  • Measure performance, benchmark where useful, and identify gaps.
  • Handle nonconformance, root causes, corrective actions, and lessons learned.
  • Maintain knowledge, decision records, metric definitions, context, and reusable operating artifacts.

Use in intake

Use this map as a starting reference for classifying source-backed business information and checking whether common capability areas have been inspected.

The map intentionally stops at common capabilities and common jobs. Business-specific intake should decide which jobs apply, what variants exist, and how the business actually handles them.

Settled: this document is a reference frame for intake classification and coverage.

Iterating: the exact capability names, job lists, and whether this reference becomes machine-readable for agent workflows.
