Visible Business Foundation

Foundation should not just document a business. It should make the business inspectable: what it can do, who or what handles the work, how the work moves, what evidence supports each claim, and where the operating system is fragile, stale, overloaded, undocumented, or improving.

Format: HTML-native Role: core concept Status: Draft

Inspectable Business

The client should not experience Foundation as a folder of documents. The better experience is: here is what your business can do, here is how each capability is handled, here is what the system depends on, and here is what the evidence proves or fails to prove.

That makes Foundation more than a knowledge base. The docs become a structured operating graph that can be read by humans, parsed by agents, checked for missing evidence, rendered into a canvas, and revised as the business changes.

Core idea: business architecture should stay connected to reality. A graph claim without a source, metric, observation, review, or explicit gap should not look settled.

Operating Graph

The recurring shape is simple enough to be durable and rich enough to expose operational detail.

01
Capability
A business outcome that must be reliably produced.
02
Job
The recurring body of work that makes the capability real.
03
Process
Steps, decisions, checks, handoffs, exceptions, and recovery.
04
Actor
Person, role, vendor, partner, customer, software, or agent.
05
Tool
Software, spreadsheet, system, asset, workflow, or data source.
06
Evidence
Source, metric, observation, record, review, or artifact.
07
Metric / Gap
What proves performance, and what is weak, unknown, or changing.

HTML remains the durable artifact format. Each artifact can carry visible prose and embedded structured metadata so the same file can be read directly, parsed by an agent, rendered into a graph, and checked for stale links or missing evidence.

Capability First

Software and non-software are both implementation details. A person doing payroll, a vendor running payroll, a spreadsheet-assisted workflow, and custom software automating payroll are different ways of handling the same capability.

Foundation should name the capability first, then inspect the current implementation: jobs, actors, processes, tools, evidence, metrics, risks, and gaps. That keeps the model useful when a process is delegated, automated, outsourced, redesigned, or brought back in-house.

The intake workspace is not the final knowledge base. It is the place where raw sources, interview answers, documents, screenshots, SaaS exports, SOPs, and observations become structured evidence and graph signals.

Visible Client Experience

The client-facing surface is the visible Foundation: a local or hosted view that lets someone inspect the business without reading every artifact first.

  • Zoomed out: capability map grouped by business outcomes, health, confidence, evidence coverage, and gaps.
  • Click a capability: show the jobs, owners or owner gaps, tools, evidence, and risks that make the capability real.
  • Click a job: show process steps, handoffs, actors, tools, evidence points, metrics, and failure/recovery paths.
  • Click an actor: show every job and capability that depends on that person, role, vendor, system, customer, or agent.
  • Click a tool: show what capabilities rely on it, what evidence it produces, and where its failure would hurt.
  • Click a metric or gap: show what it proves, what decision it supports, and what substrate should change next.

Decision Surface

The visible business graph is useful because it turns operating knowledge into decisions.

Automation

Which jobs are stable, evidenced, repeated, and safe enough to automate or agent-assist?

Dependency

Where is the business overdependent on one person, vendor, tool, undocumented process, or fragile data source?

Evidence

Which claims are supported by real sources and metrics, and which are memory, assumption, or stale documentation?

Improvement

Which capability is strategically important, weakly handled, poorly evidenced, or improving since the last review?

Operating Rules

  1. Canonical truth lives in HTML substrate and structured metadata; canvas artifacts are derived and rebuildable.
  2. Capabilities name durable outcomes; jobs and processes carry the operational detail.
  3. People, vendors, software, customers, and agents are all actor types, not separate modeling worlds.
  4. Every surfaced claim should preserve source links, confidence, freshness, and open gaps.
  5. Unknowns are first-class. The system should show fragile, stale, overloaded, undocumented, and improving areas directly.
  6. Changes discovered through the graph route back to the smallest canonical artifact that should be revised.
