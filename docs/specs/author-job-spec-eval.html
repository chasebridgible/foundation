Author Job Spec Eval

This eval judges whether an authored job spec is executable triggered work that supports a capability.

Status: active
Updated: 2026-06-06
Canonical section: #verification-contract

Verification contract

A job spec passes only when a downstream agent can understand when the job starts, who or what performs it, what process and states apply, what outputs are produced, and what evidence proves completion.

Acceptance mapping

IDConditionMust prove
JOB-1Triggered work.The job has a trigger, actor candidates, output, and exit criteria.
JOB-2Supports a capability.Registry and graph links connect the job back to its owning capability.
JOB-3Process is actionable.The process names context, tools, interfaces, states, rules, and edge cases.
JOB-4Companion specs are explicit.Technical and eval needs are linked, created, gapped, or blocked.
JOB-5Readiness questions are handled.Material ambiguity about trigger, actor, output, process, state, rule, evidence, or revision target is answered, blocked, or captured as a labeled assumption.
JOB-6No capability drift.The job does not redefine the capability or classify a standing ability inside the job lane.

Coverage plan

Manual semantic review is required for process quality. Structural evidence comes from registry and graph checks.

Commands and evidence

  • npm run spec:check
  • npm run foundation:visible-business-graph:check -- --repo .

Gaps and follow-ups

Future fixtures should cover missing triggers, generic process prose, missing state/rule/evidence details, and jobs that should have been technical or eval specs.
